### Compounding Formula

Unlike parallel strategies where capital is divided and returns averaged, sequential deployment compounds:

**Parallel (Traditional):**
- $300K split: $100K per strategy
- Each earns 50% on their allocation
- Total profit: $50K + $50K + $50K = $150K
- **Total return: $150K / $300K = 50%**

**Sequential (The Princeton Anomaly):**
- $100K rotates through all three markets
- Compounds at each step
- R_total = (1 + R₁) × (1 + R₂) × (1 + R₃) - 1
- R_total = (1.50) × (1.50) × (1.50) - 1 = **3.375 - 1 = 237.5% per daily cycle**

**Annual Compounding:**
- 252 trading days per year
- 3 markets per day = 756 compounding events
- Each profitable close immediately funds the next market open
- Result: **~240% CAGR**

> **The edge is structural, not discretionary.**

## Why Traditional Allocation Can't Compete

### Traditional Parallel Allocation Constraints:

1. **Capital Division:** $300K must be split into three $100K allocations
2. **No Cross-Market Compounding:** Each strategy operates independently
3. **Idle Capital Problem:** Money sitting in one market can't benefit from opportunities in others
4. **Linear Returns:** Three 50% strategies = 50% total return (weighted average)

### Princeton Anomaly Advantages:

1. **Full Capital Deployment:** Same $100K works three times per day
2. **Sequential Compounding:** Each market's gains fund the next market
3. **Zero Idle Capital:** Money continuously rotates through active markets
4. **Exponential Returns:** Three 50% strategies = 237.5% total return (compounded)
5. **756 Compounding Events:** vs traditional's single annual return

> **This is why we get 4.75x better capital efficiency - not from better strategies, but from better structure.**

## Risk Management Framework

### Position Sizing
- Maximum 2x leverage per market session
- Stop-loss: 3% per trade, 8.7% daily maximum
- Profit target: 50% per market cycle (realistic institutional target)

### Drawdown Cascade Protection

The sequential structure introduces compounding risk - a loss in Market 1 reduces capital available for Markets 2 and 3:

**Scenario: Early Loss**
- Nikkei: -15% → Capital drops to $85K
- DAX must recover on smaller base: +50% → $127.5K
- Nasdaq: +50% → $191.25K
- **Net result: +91.25% instead of +237.5%**

> **This is why risk management at the first market (Nikkei) is critical.**

**Mitigation:**
- Skip next market session if daily loss exceeds 5%
- Reduce position size by 50% after any losing session
- Reset to full capital allocation after 3 consecutive profitable cycles

### Maximum Daily Drawdown Limits
- Single session loss limit: -5%
- Daily aggregate loss limit: -8.7%
- Weekly circuit breaker: -15% (halt trading, review strategy)

## Why This Works: The Arbitrage Opportunity

### Market Hour Non-Overlap
- **Nikkei:** Closes before DAX opens (3:00 AM ET)
- **DAX:** Closes before Nasdaq opens (11:00 AM ET)
- **Nasdaq:** Closes before Nikkei opens (7:00 PM ET)

This creates perfect sequential deployment without margin conflicts.

### Volatility Patterns
Each market exhibits distinct volatility during its primary session:
- **Tokyo (Evening/Night ET):** Yen carry trade dynamics, Asian macro
- **Frankfurt (Early Morning ET):** European monetary policy, EUR volatility
- **New York (Daytime ET):** Fed policy, US earnings, highest volume

Different volatility drivers = low correlation = diversification benefits even while sequencing capital.
